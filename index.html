Genetic Algorithm tutorial

Possibly one of the simplest tutorials on genetic algorithms

Problem formulation

Let's consider a simple problem of maximising the sum of a bit array:

\(\max \qquad f=\sum_{i=1}^{N} x_{i} \qquad x_i \in \{0,1\} \)

The solution is obviously \( \mathbf{x}=[1, 1, 1, 1, ...] \).
Let's use genetic algorithm to find this solution.

Initiate population

Click initiate to generate a pool of random bit arrays. We will call this set of individual candidate solutions as the population.




Selection

In this step, we will pick parent individuals for crossover. We will apply selection pressure by picking from best of two. This is called "tournament" selection. Pick all individuals for the tournament and click "select" to see who wins the tournament.

Best found solution

After completing one generation, we can see what the best solution in the population. Click update best solution whenever you feel like. Finally, you can also conveniently repeat all the steps with a single click. Repeat will check if the solutions are initiated and initiate only if not done. It will also update the best solution for you. Basically you can start clicking repeat right from the start.




Hope you understand how genetic algorithms work. Cheers for trying out this tutorial!
